GO to the start of file
gg - This is the most common command. Pressing g twice quickly will move your cursor to the first character of the very first line
1G - Pressing the number 1 followed by a capital G has the same effect as gg.
:1 - Entering :1 and pressing enter will move your cursor to the fist line. This works because you are telling Vim to go to line 1.
[[ - Typing two open square brackets will also bring you to the start of the file.

Go to the end of the file
G - Pressing a capital G will move your cursor to the beginning of the last line of the file.
G$ - If you want to move to the very last character of the last line, first pres G to get the last line, then press $ to jump to the end of that line.
:$ - In command-line mode, typing :$ will take you to the last line.
]] - Typing two closed square brackets will also move your cursor to the end of the file.

View the undo history:

	press enter.

	You cn navigate this tree with the commands :earlier and :later.

Undo to a previous save
